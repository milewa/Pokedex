First Generation Games

Red and Blue

The very first Pokémon games ever made were developed by Gamefreak and published by Nintendo. Red and Blue were released in Japan in 1996 as Red and Green. Located in the fictional region Kanto, players took on the role of a 10 year-old trainer who is given a task to capture Pokémon and complete the Pokédex. Players will also encounter the villainous Team Rocket who are using the powers of Pokémon for their criminal actions. Along the journey, players have the opportunity to build up a team of 6 of their favorite Pokémon, defeat Gym Leaders, challenge the Elite Four, and ultimately become the Champion of the Kanto Region.

Yellow

The third Pokémon game releashed had a unique twist compared to it's predecessors. Inspired by the Pokémon anime, the player would start off with a Pikachu instead of the usual choice between Bulbasaur, Charmander, and Squirtle. However, it is possible for the player to receive all three of them later in the game. In addition, instead of generic Team Rockect grunts , the player fights Jessie and James. Another cool feature is Pikachu follows the trainer around and can be interacted with. This feature would not been seen again until HeartGold and SoulSilver. Other than the changes mentioned above, the plot is very similar to Red and Blue.
